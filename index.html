Chester Bennington

The one who was the idol of millions.


Here's a time line of Chester Bennington life:

  • 1976: Born on March 20 in Phoenix, Arizona.
  • 1993–1998: Fronted the grunge band Grey Daze.
  • 1996: Married his first wife, Samantha Marie Olit.
  • 1999: Joined the band Xero, which later became Hybrid Theory.
  • 2000: Released Hybrid Theory, the band's debut album, which eventually went 11-times platinum.
  • 2002: Welcomed his first son, Draven Sebastian, with Samantha.
  • 2003: Released Meteora, featuring hits like "Numb" and "Somewhere I Belong".
  • 2005: Divorced Samantha Olit and married Talinda Bentley.
  • 2007–2012: Released the albums Minutes to Midnight (2007), A Thousand Suns (2010), and Living Things (2012).
  • 2009: Released the album Out of Ashes with his side project, Dead by Sunrise.
  • 2013–2015: Joined Stone Temple Pilots as lead vocalist, replacing Scott Weiland.
  • 2013: Honored with the Stevie Ray Vaughan Award for his commitment to helping others in recovery.
  • 2017: Released Linkin Park's final album with Bennington, One More Light.
  • July 20, 2017: Passed away by suicide at the age of 41 in his home in Palos Verdes Estates, California.
  • 2020: Warner Records released a 20th-anniversary edition of Hybrid Theory.
  • 2024: Linkin Park officially reformed with new lead singer Emily Armstrong.
